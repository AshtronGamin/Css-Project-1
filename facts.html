Fun Facts About Treaty 1

  • Treaty 1 was signed in 1871, making it the first Numbered Treaty in Canada.
  • It was signed at Lower Fort Garry, near modern-day Winnipeg, Manitoba.
  • The treaty included the Anishinaabe and Swampy Cree Nations.
  • It covers much of southern Manitoba, including Winnipeg and surrounding areas.
  • Treaty 1 promised land, farming tools, and education to First Nations people.
  • Many promises made in the treaty were not fully kept by the Canadian government.
  • First Nations people saw the treaty as a way to peacefully share the land.
  • The government saw it as a way to gain control of land for settlers and farming.
  • Some chiefs did not understand that they were giving up their land forever.
  • Written in English, the treaty was not always translated correctly to Indigenous languages.
  • Treaty 1 is still important today because it affects land rights and agreements.
  • There are seven First Nations in Treaty 1 territory today.
  • These include Brokenhead, Long Plain, Peguis, Roseau River, Sagkeeng, Sandy Bay, and Swan Lake.
  • Treaty 1 is also known as “the Stone Fort Treaty.”
  • Lower Fort Garry was chosen because it was a Hudson’s Bay Company fort and a neutral location.
  • The treaty helped open the west for the Canadian Pacific Railway and new settlements.
  • Treaty 1 is a living agreement—First Nations are still fighting for the original promises to be honored.
  • August 3rd, the date of signing, is remembered every year in Treaty 1 communities.
  • The treaty included a yearly payment of $5 per person to Indigenous peoples—this amount has not increased since 1871.
  • Today, Treaty 1 is a symbol of both partnership and broken promises in Canadian history.
