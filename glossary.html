Glossary of Treaty 1 Terms

Treaty
A formal agreement between two or more groups, often countries or nations. Treaty 1 was between the Canadian government and Indigenous peoples.
First Nations
The Indigenous peoples in Canada who are neither Inuit nor Métis. They were one of the signing groups of Treaty 1.
Reserves
Lands set aside by the government for First Nations peoples to live on, often promised in treaties.
Annuity
A yearly payment made to Indigenous peoples as part of the treaty agreement.
Crown
Represents the government of Canada in treaty-making. The Crown made the treaty with Indigenous peoples.
Land Rights
The legal rights to use and live on land. Treaty 1 involved agreements about sharing and using the land.
Indigenous
The original peoples of Canada, including First Nations, Inuit, and Métis. They have unique cultures, languages, and traditions.
Colonization
The process where a country takes control of another area, often displacing the original people. Colonization deeply affected Indigenous communities.
Oral History
Stories, knowledge, and traditions passed down by speaking, not writing. Many First Nations cultures share their history this way.
Settler
A person who moves to a land that is already occupied, like the Europeans who came to Canada and signed treaties with Indigenous peoples.
Numbered Treaties
A series of 11 treaties made between the Canadian government and Indigenous groups from 1871 to 1921. Treaty 1 was the first.
Reconciliation
The process of making things right between Indigenous and non-Indigenous people, by understanding history and working together for the future.
